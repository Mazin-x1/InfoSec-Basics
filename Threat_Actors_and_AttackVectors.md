# Threat Actors & Attack Vectors  
## Vulnerability, Threat, and Risk Assessment

---

## 🧠 Core Security Concepts

Understanding security starts with three foundational elements:

### 🔓 Vulnerability
A weakness in a system (software, hardware, or process) that can be accidentally triggered or intentionally exploited to cause a security breach.

> Examples:
- Unpatched systems  
- Misconfigured services  
- Weak authentication mechanisms  

---

### ⚠️ Threat
Any potential event or actor that can exploit a vulnerability using a specific method (attack vector).

> A threat becomes real when it actively targets a system weakness.

---

### 📊 Risk
The potential impact and likelihood of a threat successfully exploiting a vulnerability.

> Risk = Probability × Impact

---

## 🧱 Common Types of Vulnerabilities
- Misconfigured systems (servers, cloud, network devices)
- Delayed security patching
- Insecure software design
- Weak or reused passwords
- Poor network segmentation

---

## 👥 Threat Actor Models

### 🌍 External Attackers
- No authorized access
- Gain entry via phishing, malware, or exploitation
- Operate remotely or physically

---

### 🏢 Internal (Insider Threats)
- Legitimate access already granted
- Misuse of privileges (intentional or accidental)

---

## 🎯 Intent vs Motivation

### Intent
What the attacker wants to achieve:
- Data theft
- System disruption
- Financial gain

### Motivation
Why they do it:
- Financial profit
- Ideology
- Curiosity or challenge
- Personal grievance

---

## 🧑‍💻 Threat Actor Categories

### 👤 Hacker
- Skill-based attacker (Black / White / Grey Hat)
- Motivation varies

### 🧒 Script Kiddie
- Uses pre-built tools without deep understanding
- Motivated by attention or experimentation

### 🧭 Hacktivist
- Ideology-driven attackers
- Examples: Anonymous-style groups

### 🏛 State-Sponsored Actor
- Highly advanced capabilities (nation-level)
- Focus: espionage, sabotage, strategic advantage

---

## ⚔️ Advanced Persistent Threat (APT)

An APT is a long-term, targeted cyberattack campaign characterized by:

- Persistent unauthorized access
- High-level stealth and sophistication
- Multi-stage attack techniques
- Targets critical sectors (energy, healthcare, government)
- Motivation: espionage or strategic gain

---

## 🚪 Common Attack Vectors

### 💻 Direct Access
Physical or local access to systems:
- Unlocked devices
- Booting from external malicious media

---

### 💾 Removable Media
- USB-based malware delivery
- Social engineering to trick user interaction

---

### 🌐 Web & Social Engineering
- Phishing websites
- Drive-by downloads
- Malicious attachments

---

### ☁️ Cloud Attacks
- Weak credentials
- Misconfigured cloud services
- Compromised APIs

---

### 📧 Email Attacks
- Phishing emails
- Malicious attachments or links
- Credential harvesting

---

### 📡 Wireless Attacks
- Wi-Fi spoofing
- Authentication cracking
- Rogue access points

---

## 📌 Key Takeaway

Security is not about isolated vulnerabilities — it is about understanding how:

> **Actors + Motivation + Attack Vector + Vulnerability = Real Risk**
